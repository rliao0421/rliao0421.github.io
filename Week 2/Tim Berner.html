Tim Berner Response

In Tim Berner's "Long Live the Web", he warns that the internet's survival depends on protecting its founding principles of universality, decentralization, and open, royality free standards. This relates to the Mozilla Manifesto. I agree with with both the readings. However I think that the Manifesto touches on important topics such as privacy and security. To preserve the internet as a platform for science and creativity, he suggests net neutrality, open data, and user control over information. Ultimately, he stresses that the internet belongs to everyone, and its future depends on everyone to keep it open and free.
